Measuring Wetland Success

A practical guide for Alberta farmers on monitoring constructed wetlands

Why Wetlands Matter

💧 Flood Control

Reduces spring runoff impacts and protects your land from flooding

🌱 Water Storage

Stores water during wet periods, releases slowly during dry seasons

🐟 Clean Water

Provides clean stock water and filters nutrients from runoff

Signs of Success

Holds Water

Stores runoff properly

Native Plants

Good vegetation established

Clear Water

Sediment settles out

Wildlife

Ducks, frogs, songbirds

Protected

Buffer zone intact

Monitoring Checklist by Year

Year 1: Establishment ▼

Structure & Water

  • Basin shape correct?
  • Water arrives when expected?
  • No erosion or settling?

Vegetation

  • Native plants germinating?
  • Bare spots present?
  • Take baseline photos
✓ Success Signs
  • Holds water after rain/snowmelt
  • Structures stable
  • Native plants growing
Year 3: Functioning ▼

Vegetation & Water

  • Native plant diversity increasing?
  • Coverage ≥75%?
  • Invasive species ≤10%?
  • Water levels consistent?

Water Quality

  • Water clearer than inlet?
  • No chronic algae?
  • Sediments settling?
✓ Success Signs
  • Distinct vegetation zones
  • Clear water after runoff
  • Wildlife increasing
Year 10: Self-Sustaining ▼

Long-Term Function

  • Water retained in wet & dry years?
  • Plant communities stable?
  • Buffer zone protecting wetland?

Maintenance

  • Sediment buildup manageable?
  • No invasive species increase?
✓ Success Signs
  • Self-sustaining with minimal intervention
  • Native plants dominant
  • Buffer zone effective
  • Can store tens of thousands of cubic meters

Key Monitoring Metrics

Water & Structures

  • Water present during expected seasons
  • Structures stable, no erosion
  • Spillways functioning properly

Vegetation & Wildlife

  • Native plant diversity increasing
  • Vegetation coverage ≥75%
  • Invasive species ≤10%

Water Quality

  • Water clarity improving
  • No chronic algae problems
  • Sediments settling properly

Wildlife Use

  • Waterfowl and songbirds present
  • Frogs and beneficial insects
  • Consistent, increasing use

Buffer Zone & Farm Management

Buffer Recommendations

  • Minimum 20 meters undisturbed vegetation
  • Reduces nutrient runoff
  • Protects from erosion and sediment

Farm Management

  • ✓ Buffer zone present and continuous
  • ✓ Manage livestock access
  • ✓ Keep nutrient sources away
